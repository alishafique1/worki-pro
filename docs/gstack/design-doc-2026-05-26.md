## Problem Statement

Homeowners in the Greater Toronto Area (Milton, Oakville, Burlington, Mississauga, Brampton) need reliable home services (HVAC, handyman, plumbing, electrical, appliance repair, smart home) but face a fragmented market: HomeStars has stale listings and paid-review problems, Jiffy has limited coverage, and Google referrals provide zero verification or concierge service. Pros (service providers) are paying $200+/month for low-quality leads on existing platforms.

TheHelper solves this with a **concierge marketplace**: consumers describe their problem once via web form or SMS → get matched with vetted, insured, background-checked pros → earn rewards on every job. Pros pay per qualified lead ($5) instead of monthly subscriptions, getting higher-intent leads.

## Status Quo

What GTA homeowners do today to find home services:

1. **HomeStars** (dominant): Search for reviews, but ~30% of reviews are paid/sponsored. Pros pay $200-500/month for "featured listings." Lead quality declining due to stale provider data. Consumers don't trust the rating system.
2. **Jiffy** (app-based): Better UX but limited GTA coverage outside Toronto core. Higher per-job fees. No phone/SMS concierge option.
3. **Google "near me" searches**: Most common first step. Zero verification. Consumer must call 3-5 pros individually, describe the problem each time, compare quotes manually. Average 45-90 minutes of coordination per job.
4. **Facebook community groups** (e.g., "Milton Moms"): Word-of-mouth referrals. No structure, no insurance verification, no dispute resolution. High trust but zero scalability.
5. **Referral from friends/family**: Gold standard but not repeatable. One person's recommendation doesn't scale to every service category.

**Cost of the status quo** (per home service job):
- **Consumer**: 45-90 min of coordination time, 3-5 phone calls, risk of hiring uninsured/vendor, no rewards or loyalty benefit for repeat usage.
- **Provider**: $200-500/mo for HomeStars with leads that are ~40% stale/non-responsive. No guarantee of exclusivity or lead quality.

TheHelper's value prop vs. status quo: "Describe once, get matched with vetted pros, earn rewards" — saving 60+ minutes per job with verified providers and a consumer loyalty loop.

## Premises

1. **The concierge model (describe once → matched) is stronger than a pure directory (browse listings).** — AGREE
   - Evidence: HomeStars' decline is driven by stale listings and paid reviews. Consumers increasingly want "take care of it for me" not "here's 50 results, good luck."
   - Counter-evidence: Bark/OddsJobs use a similar lead-gen model and have scaling challenges (lead quality, provider churn).
   - Impact: TheHelper must invest in lead quality verification (phone screening, OTP confirmation, provider verification) to differentiate from generic lead-gen.

2. **$5/lead is the right price point for launch.** — AGREE
   - Smart routing: $5 is low enough to be an impulse buy for providers, high enough to filter out non-serious providers.
   - Risk: If conversion rates (claim → book → complete) are below 20%, providers will perceive $5 as expensive for unqualified leads.
   - Mitigation: Track conversion funnel obsessively from day 1. If <15% book rate, add a manual lead qualification step before the lead feed.

3. **GTA-wide launch (all 6 categories + 5+ cities) is achievable simultaneously.** — DISAGREE — *recommend narrow wedge (HVAC × Milton)*
   - See "Narrowest Wedge" section. Launching all categories in all cities splits attention, dilutes provider-side density, and makes it harder to debug the core loop.
   - Recommended: launch HVAC in Milton on day 1, add categories weekly as provider supply builds.

4. **The reward system (points → gift cards) is a sufficient retention moat for consumers.** — AGREE, with caveat
   - Points for booking (SIGNUP_BONUS, COMPLETED_SERVICE) create a switching cost vs. going to HomeStars.
   - Caveat: points must be redeemable for tangible value within 30 days of first signup. If the first reward takes 3+ jobs to earn, the consumer never re-engages.
   - Action: Set initial reward thresholds low (100 points = $5 gift card, 1 job = ~200 points).

5. **Email-only auth is acceptable for launch.** — AGREE
   - Reduces auth complexity and surface area.
   - Trade-off: Higher friction for signup (typing email, checking inbox) vs. Google "one-click."
   - Mitigation: Passwordless email OTP codes (already built) reduce the friction of password management.

6. **Production deployment (Hostinger VPS + Cloudflare) is the #1 P0 blocker.** — AGREE
   - All product features are ~95% done. No amount of feature work matters if the site isn't live.
   - This week's priority order: (1) provision infra, (2) deploy current main branch, (3) smoke test critical path, (4) onboard 10 providers, (5) stabilize E2E tests.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Go-Live as Planned (Full GTA Launch)

**Summary**: Deploy current codebase to production across all 6 categories + 5 GTA cities. Onboard providers across all service categories simultaneously. Run the full playbook.

**Effort**: L (1-2 weeks infra + onboarding)
**Risk**: High
**Pros**:
- Captures maximum organic SEO surface area (6 categories × 5 areas = 30 landing pages already built)
- No wasted work — all existing content and pages go live immediately
- Faster path to first revenue across multiple categories
**Cons**:
- Provider supply is too thin across 6 categories — leads in non-HVAC categories sit unclaimed, creating a bad consumer experience
- Debugging is harder with 6 categories × 5 areas × multiple provider types interacting
- Support burden is higher with a wider surface area
- First impression risk: slow provider response on handyman/plumbing/electrical leads erodes trust

**Reuses**: All existing pages, operations, webhook integrations, reward system

### Approach B: Narrow Wedge (HVAC × Milton First)

**Summary**: Launch with HVAC only in Milton, ON. Onboard 5-10 HVAC pros this week. Focus all marketing (Google Ads, Facebook, flyers) on HVAC + Milton. Verify the unit economics before expanding.

**Effort**: S (3-5 days infra + focused onboarding)
**Risk**: Low-Med
**Pros**:
- Concentrated provider supply ensures fast response times (leads claimed within minutes, not days)
- Clean feedback loop: one category, one city, one funnel to optimize
- First 10 providers become reference accounts for recruiting the next 40
- Easier to debug — errors in lead routing, claiming, billing are category-isolated
- Marketing spend is highly targeted (HVAC queries in Milton)
**Cons**:
- Other category landing pages sit in "coming soon" state — may confuse organic visitors
- Handyman/plumbing/electrical providers onboarded later may feel deprioritized
- SEO investment in non-HVAC category pages gives no immediate return
- Slight delay in total addressable market capture

**Reuses**: HVAC pages already polished; lead feed filters by category; provider verification pipeline is category-agnostic

### Approach C: Concierge-First (Phone/SMS-Heavy Launch)

**Summary**: Emphasize the SMS/phone concierge channel over the web self-serve. Market a phone number ("Text TheHelper at 555-HELPER") as the primary entry point. Humans (or AI) triage requests, match to providers manually. The web form exists but is secondary.

**Effort**: M (telephony infra, staffing/automation)
**Risk**: Med
**Pros**:
- Higher-touch experience builds trust fast — "a real person helped me"
- Phone/SMS bypasses web SEO competition — meets customers where they already are
- Twilio integration + phone/SMS pipeline already built (twilioCallSid, callRecordingUrl, channel: PHONE)
- Callers are higher-intent leads (calling = immediate need)
- Differentiation vs. HomeStars (pure web directory) is maximized
**Cons**:
- Not scalable without staffing or AI concierge — per-call handling cost is high
- Less SEO leverage — web content doesn't generate organic traffic if phone is primary channel
- Requires on-call concierge (even automated SMS flow needs monitoring)
- Slower growth curve vs. web-self-serve + SEO
- Multiple consumer personas: younger homeowners prefer web self-serve, older homeowners prefer phone

**Reuses**: Twilio webhook handlers, SMS OTP flow, communication log system, lead capture pipeline

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B: Narrow Wedge (HVAC × Milton)** — because the most dangerous failure mode for a marketplace is thin supply. By concentrating on one category × one city, TheHelper guarantees a fast, reliable provider response for every consumer lead. That first-hand experience ("I described my furnace problem and a verified HVAC pro called me back in 10 minutes") is the only thing that creates the word-of-mouth loop that drives organic growth. Full GTA launch risks the opposite: slow responses in thin categories that permanently tag TheHelper as "tried it, no one called back."

**Implementation plan**:
1. **Week 1 (this week)**: Provision Hostinger VPS + Cloudflare. Deploy current main. Smoke-test: lead submit → OTP → lead feed → claim → book → complete → review. Onboard 5 HVACK Milton pros via direct outreach.
2. **Week 2**: Onboard 5 more HVACK Milton pros (total 10). Verify unit economics: lead claim rate, booking rate, completion rate. Fix any funnel drops >30%.
3. **Week 3**: Expand to Handyman in Milton. Onboard 5 handyman pros. Apply HVAC learnings.
4. **Week 4**: HVAC + Handyman in Oakville. Onboard pros in new area.
5. **Week 5+**: Add plumbing, electrical, appliance repair, smart home — one category per week as provider supply confirms.

## Open Questions

1. **Lead-to-booking conversion**: What is the actual conversion rate from lead claim to booked appointment? Target >20% to justify $5/lead. This must be measured within the first 50 leads.
2. **Consumer acquisition channel**: Is the primary channel SEO (organic "Milton HVAC" searches), Google Ads, Facebook, or referral? The landing pages are SEO-optimized, but paid acquisition may be needed to jump-start the marketplace.
3. **Provider onboarding capacity**: Can the team manually onboard 10 providers per week? Each provider requires: licence verification, insurance check, TSSA/WSIB validation, phone interview, slug creation, Cal.com setup. This is a bottleneck.
4. **Reward economics**: What's the cost per point redeemed? If 200 points = $5 gift card and each job earns 200 points, the cost per job is $5 + platform fees. Need to ensure the math works at scale.
5. **Stripe live keys**: Are Stripe live keys configured, or is pricing disabled until they are? This is a P1 blocker per the task context.

## Distribution Plan

TheHelper is a web service — consumers access via browser, providers access via browser dashboard. No downloadable binary. No CI/CD pipeline is currently configured; deployment is manual via Hostinger VPS.

**Recommended deployment pipeline**:
1. GitHub → push to main
2. Manual `git pull` on Hostinger VPS
3. `wasp build` → serves built artifacts via Node.js/Express
4. Cloudflare proxies all traffic (DDoS protection, caching, SSL)

**Future**: Set up GitHub Actions for auto-deploy on main merge. Not required for launch but should be the first post-launch engineering improvement.

## The Assignment

**Onboard one real HVAC provider in Milton this week.** Not "start the process." Get them fully verified: licence uploaded, insurance confirmed, slug created, Cal.com connected, lead feed visible to them. Then watch them claim a real lead. That's the loop.

Don't try to onboard 10 providers at once. One. Make it work for Mike. Then use his experience to recruit the next 9.
